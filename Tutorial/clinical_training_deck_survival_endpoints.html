Background: Clinical Endpoints

Understanding the distinction between time-to-event and response endpoints is crucial for correct analysis.

  • OS (Overall Survival): Time from randomization to death from any cause.
  • PFS (Progression-Free Survival): Time from randomization to disease progression or death.
  • DOCR (Duration of Complete Response): Time from first documented CR to disease progression or death.
  • DOR (Duration of Response): Time from first documented response (CR or PR) to disease progression or death.
Crucial Population Difference:
OS and PFS analyses utilize the Full Analysis Set (FAS)—meaning all randomized patients.
DOR and DOCR analyses use ONLY Responders (CR+PR or CR only) as the denominator.

Standard Efficacy Table Example

A typical summary of time-to-event endpoints (e.g., PFS) comparing two arms.

Metric Generic Drug A (N=150) Control (N=150)
Number of Events, n (%) 85 (56.7%) 110 (73.3%)
Number of Censored, n (%) 65 (43.3%) 40 (26.7%)
Min / Max (Months) 1.5 / 42.8+ 0.9 / 36.5+
Median Follow-Up (Reverse KM) 24.5 months 22.1 months
Median PFS (Standard KM) 14.2 months 8.6 months
12-month Landmark Rate 62.4% 41.2%

Interpreting the Data

The '+' Symbol

In Min/Max ranges (e.g., 42.8+), the '+' indicates the observation is censored. This means at the last follow-up, the patient had not experienced the event (still alive/responding without progression).

'NR' (Not Reached)

Often seen in Median survival outputs. It means the Kaplan-Meier curve has not yet dropped below 50%. The data is not mature enough to calculate a median.

⚠️ The "Two Denominators Trap"

When reporting Event rates, the denominator is the Total Population (or Total Responders for DOR). However, when summarizing the 'Reasons for Censoring', the denominator must be the Total Censored patients, not the total population.

Data Structure & CDISC Setup

Essential Dates to Collect:

  • Scan dates (Imaging)
  • Progression dates (Investigator assessed)
  • Death dates
  • New anti-cancer therapy start dates

Standard CDISC Datasets:

  • ADSL: Subject-Level Analysis Dataset. Contains demographics, treatment groups, and population flags (e.g., FASFL).
  • ADEFFTTE: Efficacy Time-to-Event Dataset. Contains the analysis value (AVAL) in months/days, and the censoring variable (CNSR) where 0 = Event and 1 = Censored.
  • ADRS: Response Dataset. Used to derive best overall response (BOR) and filter responders for DOR analysis.

Inspiring Research Questions

For MDs pursuing Master's or publishing papers, defining a sharp statistical question is step one.

  1. Comparative Efficacy:
    Does Generic Drug A significantly improve PFS compared to the Standard Control in the first-line setting?

  2. Landmark Analysis:
    What is the 12-month Overall Survival (OS) rate specifically within high-risk subgroups (e.g., patients with liver metastases)?

  3. Surrogacy / Predictive Modeling:
    Does achieving an early Duration of Response (DOR) > 6 months predict long-term Overall Survival (OS)?

Statistical Methods: KM vs Reverse KM

Standard Kaplan-Meier (KM)

Used to estimate actual survival or progression time.

  • Estimates time to the event of interest.
  • Coding: Event = 0, Censored = 1 (Standard CDISC mapping).

Reverse Kaplan-Meier

Used to estimate Median Follow-Up time, indicating the maturity of the clinical trial data.

  • Estimates the time patients are observed before dropping out or cutting off data.
  • Coding: The censoring indicator is flipped. Actual events become censored (1), and actual censored patients become events (0).

Programming Code Snippets

How to generate standard Kaplan-Meier estimates across different software.

SAS

proc lifetest data=adefftte time=aval*cnsr(1);
    strata grp;
run;

R

library(survival)
fit <- survfit(Surv(aval, event) ~ grp, data=data)
print(fit)

SPSS (Syntax)

KM aval BY grp
  /STATUS=event(1)
  /PRINT MEAN.

QC & Regulatory (RA) Submission Checklist

Before submitting tables for publication or regulatory review, ensure the following:

  • ✅ Denominators Match: Verify that the N counts in efficacy tables perfectly match the population flags in the ADSL dataset.
  • ✅ Censoring Hierarchies: Confirm that the rules for censoring (e.g., missing 2+ scans, starting new therapy) align strictly with FDA/EMA guidelines.
  • ✅ Missing Data: Check the handling of missing baseline disease assessments and post-baseline scans.
  • ✅ Formatting: Ensure proper conditional formatting for Min/Max values (the '+' symbol must accurately reflect censoring status at the maximum follow-up).
